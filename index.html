Computer Viruses

What are computer viruses?

A computer virus is a piece of harmful software commonly found online. “Computer viruses” is actually a broad term for many different types of harmful software. A virus is a piece of software that is hidden inside a larger program. The first computer virus was written in 1971. Early in the history of computer development, some people thought of electronic infections as relatively harmless, high-tech pranks.

What kinds of Computer Viruses are there?

There are multiple sub-categories of malware. The most commonly known virus is a document-based virus. A Trojan horse virus is disguised as another file that may appear safe. Most Trojan horse viruses are installed without the user knowing but the virus will do a lot of damage. Trojan horses is a program that does something undocumented. A macro is a piece of code that can be embedded in a data file. Worms are very similar to viruses in that they are computer programs that replicate functional copies of themselves. Because of their similarity to viruses, worms also are often referred to as viruses. One variation on the computer virus is the e-mail virus, which spreads quickly.The two most common kinds of attacks and the ones best known by the public are Trojan horses and worms.
